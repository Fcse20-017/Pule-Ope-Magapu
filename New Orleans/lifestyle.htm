Lifestyle in New Orleans

MERCEDES-BENZ SUPERDOME

Located in the heart of the New Orleans, the Mercedes-Benz Superdome has captivated audiences from all over the world hosting Super Bowls, Final Fours, major concerts, Monster Jam, and festivals of all kinds. And with a stunning spot on the New Orleans skyline and its iconic design, the Superdome is one of America’s most recognizable landmarks.

NOLA MOTORSPORTS PARK

Are you a racing fan or a racer or a pit crew team member? Whatever category you fall into you'll find plenty of high-speed action at the NOLA Motorsports Park in Avondale, LA, just 14 miles from downtown New Orleans. The 700-acre facility opened its gates at the beginning of 2012 and has seen plenty of action since.

SMOOTHIE KING CENTER

The building is home to the NBA Pelicans, and has hosted multiple NBA All-Star Games and NCAA Women’s Final Fours. Numerous special events and general sessions have been held with outstanding results, within the same walls where champions have been crowned and world-class performers have thrilled thousands of fans.

Neighborhood Guide

New Orleans is an eclectic mix of neighborhoods - from the funky, bohemian Bywater all the way to the oak-lined Garden District. There's something for every type of traveler in each part of town, whether you're seeking authentic live music, historic (and quite photogenic) architecture, or a vibrant nightlife scene.

STREETS TO VISIT

Tied directly to history, some street names appear in French on signs in the French Quarter, with the street’s Spanish title immortalized in ceramic tile on the sides of buildings on corners (Calle del Maine, Calle del Arsenal). Streets named for saints (St. Ann and St. Peter) often parallel avenues honoring French royals (Conti), a class not particularly known for its moral high ground.
